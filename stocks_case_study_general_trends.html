Case Study: A Preliminary Side-By-Side Analysis of the Contractors

So far in our presentation, we have dived into the individual contractors' tendencies with respect to price, return, and volume. However, what if we want to conduct a more in-depth investigation on these trends jointly?

We have now arrived at a case study of the five contractors from 1985 to 2021. To begin, let's evaluate the contractors' annual returns, but from a different perspective. As mentioned earlier, a stock's annual return is calculated as the relative change from its first opening price to its last closing one within the same year. Applying this definition, we can determine which contractors performed better as investments over time. We should also take the time to compare their average closing prices and volumes on a daily basis.


Annual and Average Returns

The combo chart above reports the contractors' annual returns from 1985 to 2021 and sorts them by their means. Notably, while General Dynamics in 1991 reported the highest annual return (110.78%) for any year within our time span, it placed third among the stocks for its mean annual return on its investment (12.05%). Boeing (14.24%) and Lockheed Martin (12.29%) posted higher mean returns. Note as well how despite the fact that Raytheon Technologies returned the lowest on average (11.44%), it actually dropped in its investment for fewer years than the others.


Average Closing Prices and Daily Volumes

Clearly, a stock's average closing price or volume does not necessarily correlate with its average annual return. This discrepancy is clear when comparing Boeing and Lockheed Martin, as the latter closed on average at a higher price daily but returned less on average annually. In addition, while Raytheon Technologies offered the lowest annual return from 1985 to 2021, and closed on average lower than the other contractors, its average daily volume was the highest. This suggests that its stock is relatively more liquid, allowing its investors to trade more frequently throughout the day. Furthermore, any news about its financial status, positive or negative, are likely to have a more significant effect on its price than the others.
